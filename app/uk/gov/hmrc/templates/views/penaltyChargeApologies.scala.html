We have charged you a penalty by mistake

On 9 December 2020, we charged you a penalty by mistake. We are sorry about this, and for any inconvenience that it may have caused you.

The penalty was for your 2018 to 2019 Self Assessment tax return being 12 months late. We have now cancelled that penalty, and you do not have to pay it, because your return is not 12 months late.

About other penalties we have charged you

The other penalties we have charged you for not filing your tax return are correct. This includes the initial penalty for your return being late, as well as the penalty for your return being 6 months late.

We explained more about the penalties that you may be charged, and when we might charge them, in our letter dated 18 November 2020.

What you need to do next

If you have:
  • not yet paid the other penalties, please pay them as soon as you can
  • not yet filed your 2018 to 2019 Self Assessment tax return, please file it now - we may charge you a penalty if your return is 12 months late (it will be 12 months late on 1 February 2021)
  • filed your return but not yet paid what you owe for that return, please pay as soon as you can or contact us to agree a payment arrangement

The section below tells you about help that’s available if you’re having problems paying.

Help if you think you’ll have problems paying

If you think you’ll have problems paying what you owe for your 2018 to 2019 Self Assessment tax return, you can ask us for a ‘time to pay’ arrangement. This lets you pay what you owe in instalments. A time to pay arrangement can include everything you owe us, including penalties and interest.

If you want a time to pay arrangement that includes the tax you owe for your 2018 to 2019 return, you’ll need to file your return first. So please do it as soon as you can.

You should then phone our loan charge helpline on 03000 599 110 (Monday to Friday 8.30am to 5pm, excluding bank holidays). We can discuss your options with you and agree an affordable time to pay arrangement.

If you need extra support

If you have any health or personal circumstances that may make it difficult for you to deal with us, please tell us. We’ll help you in whatever way we can. For more information about this, either click here, or go to www.gov.uk and search for ‘get help from HMRC if you need extra support’.

More information about the loan charge

You can find more information about the loan charge and your reporting responsibilities in our online guidance. You can click on the links below, or go to www.gov.uk and search for:

  • ‘report and account for your disguised remuneration loan charge’
  • ‘find out how the changes to the loan charge affect you’

If you have a tax adviser

If you have a tax adviser, you may want to talk to them about this message. If you do not have one, free advice is available from organisations such as TaxAid and Citizens Advice.

TaxAid can give tax advice to people on low incomes. For example, they can help check that a tax bill is right and deal directly with us, if needed. Citizens Advice can help people work out ways to pay the tax they owe. You can find their contact details by searching online.
